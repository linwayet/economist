The article challenges the popular narrative that artificial intelligence data centres are primarily responsible for rising electricity bills in America. While data centres—concentrated in places like Ashburn, Virginia—are indeed consuming more power, the article argues that their share of total electricity demand remains modest: less than 10% today, and projected to reach only about a fifth by 2030. Research from the Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory confirms that data-centre load was not the main driver of rate increases between 2019 and 2024. Instead, the article identifies grid infrastructure upgrades, rising costs of power equipment and raw materials, the need to harden grids against extreme weather, and increasing domestic competition for natural gas as the true culprits. Significantly, electricity prices began rising in early 2021, nearly two years before ChatGPT's launch. Furthermore, the article argues that big technology companies may actually be lowering prices for consumers by investing in new energy capacity, making their data centres more grid-flexible, and adding large-scale demand that enables utilities to spread fixed costs across a wider base. The counterintuitive conclusion: Americans should demand more AI, not less.

美國電費正在上漲，民眾怒火中燒。他們知道該怪誰——那些如饑似渴地為人工智慧數據中心汲取更多電力的科技公司老闆。阿什本是維吉尼亞州一個有4.5萬人口的平凡小鎮，因坐擁約150個數據中心而贏得「數據中心大道」的稱號。這些數據中心消耗的電力相當於費城（一個有160萬人口的城市）。3月4日，唐納德·川普召集科技業領袖，簽署了一份承諾「建設、引進或自行購買電力供應……確保美國人的電費不會上漲」的宣言。
儘管有這些莊嚴的承諾，但各公司執行長對遏制電費幾乎無能為力。然而，這並非因為AI是不可阻擋的。而是因為AI熱潮並非電費上漲的主要原因。
近年來，零售電費的確跑贏了整體通膨。數據中心確實在消耗更多電力。高盛估計，未來幾年，數據中心將佔美國整體需求增長的近一半。
然而，即使是最樂觀的預測，也將數據中心在2030年的電力需求佔比定為僅五分之一。今天則不到十分之一。勞倫斯伯克利國家實驗室去年發布的一項研究顯示，在截至2024年的五年間，數據中心負載並非電費上漲的主因。研究將矛頭指向電網升級，以及發電設備和銅等原材料成本上升。伍德麥肯錫（Wood Mackenzie）估計，去年配電變壓器的需求超過供應10%，電力變壓器的缺口更達30%。製造商表示，關鍵電網設備的等待清單長達120週以上，相比2021年的50週大幅延長。
許多電費早在2021年初便開始上漲，比ChatGPT的問世早了將近兩年，從而引發AI熱潮。電費的上漲趨勢不僅如此，預計還將因非AI原因持續。代表私營電力公用事業的愛迪生電氣研究所（Edison Electric Institute）預測，其成員2025至2029年的累計資本支出將達1.1兆美元，高於前五年的7,650億美元。超過一半用於配電和輸電基礎設施的資金，將用於更換老舊設備，以及應對氣候變化加劇的極端天氣所帶來的安全強化需求。2019年至2023年間，加州大型公用事業公司僅在降低野火風險方面便花費了270億美元。這些投資長期被忽視。一位業界大佬表示，如今AI為贏得監管機構批准、將成本轉嫁給消費者提供了一個藉口。

此外，還有其他非AI的成本壓力。即使在伊朗戰爭推升天然氣價格之前，分析師就已預測，隨著更多液化天然氣出口終端投入運營，國內買家將與外國買家的競爭愈發激烈。惯於懷疑再生能源的川普，通過阻礙太陽能和風力發電容量的增長而雪上加霜。布拉特爾集團顧問公司的Peter Fox-Penner指出，這導致最廉價的新型發電形式無謂地抬高了電費。
AI甚至可能在壓低電費。科技巨頭已在投資自有產能（主要是清潔能源，雖然大家都心知肚明）。微軟已簽署長期協議，重啟三哩島的一座核反應爐，為其數據中心供電。Meta已資助多家核能新創企業。去年12月，Google的母公司Alphabet以50億美元收購了Intersect Power，一家效用規模太陽能發電和電池儲能開發商。阿什本一個屬於大型運營商Equinix的數據中心，正在試驗燃料電池技術。
除了增加自有供電能力，大型科技公司還在提升現有產能的靈活性。Google已與中西部電力公用事業公司印第安納密西根電力公司（Indiana Michigan Power）達成新型費率安排，讓其數據中心在其他用電高峰時段降低消耗。微軟走得更遠。在其愛爾蘭的一個數據中心，它將備用電池用作「電網穩定器」——在電網壓力時段既可向電網輸電，也可從電網吸收多餘電力。由於電網的運行負載往往遠低於滿載，引入一個大型的靈活客戶，既能為電力公用事業帶來大量收入，又無需代價高昂的擴容。這讓電力公用事業在保持利潤率的同時，降低居民用電費率。
電力研究院（Electric Power Research Institute）這家智庫發現，一些電力需求在2019年至2024年間快速增長的州，調整通膨後，電費反而有所下降。世界資源研究所（World Resources Institute）另一家智庫指出，在北達科他州，石油天然氣開採、加密貨幣礦工、數據中心運營商和食品加工商帶動的需求增長，使當地電力用戶的電費大幅下降。加州大型電力公用事業公司PG&E估計，每增加1吉瓦的用電負載，電費就可降低2%。若美國人真想要更低的電費，他們應該大聲疾呼要更多的AI，而不是更少。■

Finance & economics | A load of nonsense
Americans’ electricity bills are up. Don’t blame AI Were it not for data centres, prices might be even higher

彩色多頁排版主題：奶茶暖調
Power bills are going up in America and the people are angry. They know whom to blame—the bosses of technology firms thirsting for more juice to fuel artificial-intelligence data centres. Ashburn, a town of 45,000 in a featureless part of Virginia that has earned the nickname “Data Centre Alley”, has some 150 of these. They consume roughly as much electricity as Philadelphia, a city of 1.6m. On March 4th Donald Trump convened tech leaders to sign a pledge to “build, bring or buy their own power supply…ensuring that Americans’ electricity bills will not increase”. Their solemn pledges notwithstanding, the chief executives can do little to contain prices. That is not, though, because ai is unstoppable. It is because the ai boom is not chiefly to blame for the rising costs. In the past few years retail electricity prices have indeed outpaced overall inflation (see chart 1). And data centres are gobbling up more power. Goldman Sachs, a bank, reckons that they will account for nearly half of the overall demand growth in America in the coming years. Yet even bullish forecasts put data centres’ share of total demand at only a fifth in 2030. Today it is less than a tenth. A study last year by the Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory showed that data-centre load was not the main cause of the rate rises in the five years to 2024. It fingered grid upgrades and rising costs of power-generating equipment and raw materials such as copper. Wood Mackenzie, a research firm, estimates that last year demand for distribution transformers outstripped supply by 10%. For power transformers the gap was 30%. Manufacturers report waiting lists for essential grid-related kit stretching to 120 weeks or more, up from 50 weeks in 2021.

chart: the economist Many prices started going up in early 2021, nearly two years before the launch of Chatgpt ignited the ai boom. They are likely to keep rising for non-ai reasons. The Edison Electric Institute, which represents private-sector utilities, predicts its members’ cumulative capital spending will reach $1.1trn between 2025 and 2029, up from $765bn in the previous five years. More than half the sum for distribution and transmission infrastructure will go on replacing ageing equipment and hardening it against extreme weather made likelier by climate change. Between 2019 and 2023 big Californian utilities spent $27bn just on mitigating wildfire risk. These investments have been neglected for years. Now, says an industry bigwig, ai provides a pretext to help win approval from regulators to pass the cost on to consumers. And these are not the only non-ai cost pressures. Even before the war in Iran caused natural-gas prices to rise, analysts were predicting that domestic buyers would be increasingly competing with foreign ones as more export terminals for liquefied natural gas come online. Mr Trump, an inveterate renewables sceptic, has not helped by impeding the growth of solar and wind capacity. Peter Fox-Penner of the Brattle Group, a consultancy, notes that as a result prices are rising needlessly for the cheapest forms of new power generation. ai may even be lowering prices. The tech giants are already investing in their own capacity (mostly, whisper it, in the clean variety). Microsoft has signed a long-term deal to restart a nuclear reactor at Three Mile Island to supply its data centres. Meta has backed a handful of nuclear startups. In December Google’s corporate parent, Alphabet, paid $5bn for Intersect Power, a developer of utility-scale solar power and battery storage. A data centre in Ashburn belonging to Equinix, a big operator, is experimenting with fuel cells. Besides adding its own supply, big tech is making existing capacity more flexible. Google has agreed to novel tariff arrangements with Indiana Michigan Power, a midwestern utility, whereby its data centres can reduce their consumption when other demand is high. Microsoft is going further. In one of its Irish data centres it uses backup batteries as a “grid stabiliser” that can push power back into the network or draw excess power from it at times of stress. Since grids often run well below full capacity, adding a large, flexible customer can bring in lots of revenue for utilities without requiring costly expansion. This lets the utilities lower rates for households while preserving their margins. The Electric Power Research Institute, a think-tank, found that some states with high load growth between 2019 and 2024 reported price declines, after adjusting for inflation (see chart 2). The World Resources Institute, another think-tank, notes that in North Dakota rising demand from oil and gas extraction, cryptocurrency miners, data-centre operators and food-processors led to large price reductions for local electricity users. pg&e, a big Californian utility, estimates that adding a gigawatt of load could lower bills by up to 2%. If Americans want lower electricity bills, they should be shouting for more ai, not less. ■
